Who We Are

NiostGroup is structured as a holding-led group with aligned operating entities. The group exists to create a controlled, scalable and governable commercial structure in which procurement, logistics, partnerships and future operational activities can be managed with consistency across countries and entities.

The holding is not intended to function as a passive registration layer. It exists to set standards, define governance, control procurement principles, protect group interests, and make sure each entity operates within a unified framework. This creates consistency in supplier engagement, compliance, documentation, commercial positioning and operational execution.

Positionering

Holding-led, standards-driven, multi-entity and governance-based.

Praktische boodschap

NiostGroup functioneert als een gecontroleerd groepsmodel, niet als losse entiteiten zonder centrale aansturing.

Why We Exist

NiostGroup exists to build a reliable and scalable group structure for commercial cooperation, supplier engagement and operational delivery. The purpose is not merely to buy goods or services, but to create a procurement-led system in which each engagement supports quality, continuity, transparency and strategic growth.

The group model exists because fragmented procurement creates inconsistency, risk and weak control. A structured operating model allows NiostGroup to engage partners professionally, apply common standards, reduce risk and scale with discipline across entities and countries.

Core rationale

Procurement is hier niet alleen purchasing; het is een controlemechanisme voor kwaliteit, continuïteit en schaalbare samenwerking.

Group Structure

The group is organized around a holding that provides direction, governance and oversight, supported by entities that perform execution within defined mandates. This structure allows strategic control to remain centralized while operational activities can be carried out in the most suitable entity or country context.

Each entity should therefore be understood not as an isolated business, but as a controlled part of a wider procurement and operating system. The holding defines the rules, the entities perform within those rules, and all material decisions must remain traceable and documented.

LayerPurposeControl logic

Holding Governance

The holding must govern procurement through formal policy, approval authority, ethical standards, supplier due diligence requirements, and documentation control. It must define which procurements are centralized, which are delegated, which approvals are required, and when issues must be escalated.

The holding must also ensure that no procurement activity takes place without integrity safeguards. Conflict-of-interest rules, anti-bribery expectations, supplier declarations, and due diligence checks must be mandatory before strategic or material engagements proceed.

Governance controls

  • Policy ownership
  • Approval thresholds
  • Escalation rules
  • Conflict-of-interest controls
  • Documentation retention

What must be visible

  • Which spend is delegated
  • Which spend requires holding approval
  • Which cases trigger legal or compliance review
  • Which records are mandatory for audit trail

Entity Procurement Model

Each entity operates procurement inside the framework created by the holding. This means entities may identify needs, gather specifications, support sourcing, and manage local execution, but they do not create their own uncontrolled procurement logic.

Entity procurement must therefore follow the same core sequence: requirement definition, internal review, sourcing support, supplier evaluation, due diligence, approval, contracting, onboarding, and supplier performance monitoring.

Entity roleAllowed activityBoundary
Local managementNeed identification and market inputCannot bypass group controls
Entity procurement supportSpecification, sourcing support, vendor contactUses approved templates and approval path
OperationsReceipt, service confirmation, performance feedbackMust document delivery and issues

Before Partner Approach

Before any partner is approached, NiostGroup must already have its internal pack in order. A procurement officer should never start external conversations before the group can clearly explain its identity, structure, governance, entity logic, cooperation scope and decision path.

Minimum readiness

  • Clear company description
  • Group and entity structure
  • Procurement scope
  • Governance narrative
  • Decision ownership

Before outreach

  • Supplier intake criteria
  • Onboarding logic
  • Basic compliance set
  • Due diligence readiness
  • Defined purpose of discussion

Partner Dossier Pack

For every serious external partner approach, a standard pack should exist. This pack should allow the partner to understand who NiostGroup is, what type of cooperation is proposed, how decisions are governed, and what documentation and standards are expected before onboarding begins.

PR and Positioning

The procurement officer must be able to explain NiostGroup in one disciplined narrative. The message should not sound improvised or purely commercial. It should communicate structure, seriousness, trust, control and long-term intent.

Suggested positioning line

Approved narrative

NiostGroup is a holding-led group structure designed to manage procurement, partnerships and operational execution through a disciplined governance model, clear entity roles and controlled partner engagement.
